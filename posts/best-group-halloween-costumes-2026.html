Best Group Halloween Costumes 2026

Ten coordinated looks your whole crew will actually want to wear.

Group costumes are the ultimate Halloween flex. When your whole crew shows up coordinated — and everyone is actually committed — it stops the party cold in the best possible way. The hard part is finding a theme everyone can agree on, characters that cover the whole group, and pieces that are actually available to order before Halloween.

We've done the legwork. The picks below cover a range of group sizes (most work for 3–8 people), themes, and budgets. We've prioritized sets where every person in the group gets a distinct, recognizable character — because nothing deflates a group costume like two people showing up as the same guy.

The 10 Best Group Costumes for 2026

01 — Best for TV Fans

The Office (US Cast)

Michael Scott's world is endlessly recognizable, which makes it perfect for a group costume. All you really need are the right name badges and character-specific props. Michael brings the "World's Best Boss" mug, Dwight wears his volunteer sheriff's badge and a beet (yes, really), Jim rocks his dead-eyed business casual, Pam brings the reception desk energy, and Kevin shows up with a soup pot. The costumes themselves are everyday office wear — the comedy comes entirely from the props and character commitment. This one rewards the group that can stay in character.

You can buy the official name badges and Dunder Mifflin accessories as a bundle, or piece it together item by item.

02 — Best Retro TV Pick

Stranger Things — The Hawkins Gang

Stranger Things costumes have staying power because the visuals are so strong. For a group of four to six, go with: Eleven in her pink dress with the bloody nose (or her Season 4 Vecna-hunter look), Mike in his classic red jacket, Dustin in his Camp Know Where gear, Joyce clutching her Christmas lights, Hopper in his police chief uniform, and Steve with the infamous bat wrapped in nails. The 1980s setting means most of the base clothing is easy to source from thrift stores, and the character-defining accessories are all available on Amazon.

03 — Best Classic Movie Group

The Wizard of Oz

Few group costumes are as universally recognized as The Wizard of Oz. Dorothy's gingham dress and ruby slippers, the Scarecrow's patched overalls and floppy hat, the Tin Man's silver suit and funnel hat, the Cowardly Lion's mane — these are iconic silhouettes that read across a crowded party from 30 feet away. For a fifth person, Glinda the Good Witch in her pink ball gown rounds out the ensemble perfectly. Complete character costume sets for all five leads are available, making this one of the easiest large-group picks to execute.

04 — Best Thriller Group

Squid Game Players

The Squid Game aesthetic has become a genuine Halloween staple: the teal-green tracksuits with white number patches are clean, minimal, and terrifying in the right context. For a group of four or more, each person wears the same tracksuit but with a different three-digit number. Add white slip-on shoes and a white mask for the guards' look, or pair a few players with a Front Man in his black suit and mask for the full hierarchy effect. These costumes are comfortable enough to wear all night, which puts them ahead of most of the competition.

05 — Best for Mixed Groups

The Avengers

The Avengers work for a group because there's a character for every personality. Iron Man's red-and-gold suit is a statement. Captain America appeals to people who want to look heroic without looking ridiculous. Thor's costume doubles as a conversation piece. Black Widow is one of the most popular individual women's costumes regardless of group theme. Hulk works best as a last-minute decision (green paint and ripped shorts). The breadth of the roster also means late additions to your group can slot in as Hawkeye, Scarlet Witch, or Spider-Man without breaking the theme.

06 — Best 90s Throwback

Spice Girls

The Spice Girls remain one of the most fun five-person group costumes available, and the looks are as distinct as it gets. Sporty Spice in the Umbro tracksuit. Scary Spice in the animal print. Baby Spice in the pink mini and platform sneakers. Posh Spice in the black dress and stilettos. Ginger Spice in the Union Jack mini. The 90s revival has made most of these pieces easy to find, and the beauty of this costume is that the characters are defined by vibe and accessories as much as specific items — which means more room for creative interpretation.

07 — Best Addams Family

The Addams Family

Wednesday Addams has had a massive resurgence, and the full Addams Family ensemble is one of the most striking group costumes you can pull off. Wednesday's black dress with white collar is iconic and widely available. Gomez in his pinstripe suit and pencil mustache, Morticia in her draped black gown, Uncle Fester in his bald cap and black robe, Pugsley in his striped shirt — every character is distinct and the palette is unified (all black, all the time). This one works for families with kids, but it's equally great for an adult friend group that loves a dark aesthetic.

08 — Best Animated Group

Scooby-Doo is one of the most reliably crowd-pleasing group costumes going. 09 — Best Decade Theme

1970s Disco Group

If your group struggles to agree on a single IP, a decade theme is the cleanest solution — and disco is the best of them. 10 — Best for Smaller Groups

Grease — Pink Ladies (or T-Birds)

Grease splits beautifully into two group costume tracks: the Pink Ladies and the T-Birds. The Pink Ladies are the stronger choice — the matching satin jackets in hot pink are immediately recognizable and look great together as a group of four. Sandy, Rizzo, Frenchie, and Jan each bring a distinct personality to the look. The T-Birds work just as well: matching leather jackets, white t-shirts, and slicked-back hair. For a mixed group, combining both gives you one of the most iconic ensembles in film history. Satin Pink Ladies jackets are available as a set and are actually warm enough to wear for outdoor trick-or-treating.

How to Pick the Right Group Costume

The single biggest mistake groups make is choosing a theme first and assigning characters second. Go the other direction: figure out who is actually willing to commit to a full costume, then pick a theme that covers that exact headcount with characters each person genuinely wants to be. A group of four committed people will always beat a group of eight where half are wearing only a vague gesture toward the theme.

Order early. Amazon Prime delivery has spoiled a lot of people into thinking they can order a costume on October 29th. That works for individual pieces, but coordinated group costumes — especially those involving matching jackets, uniform sets, or specific accessories — can go out of stock fast once October hits. If you've landed on a theme, order by mid-September for the best selection.

Size up for comfort. Most Halloween costume sizing runs narrow and short. If you're planning to wear your costume all night — through dinner, dancing, outdoor events — order a size larger than your normal fit. It's a lot easier to take in extra fabric than to spend the night unable to breathe.
